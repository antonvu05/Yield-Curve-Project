What is a weight? Each factor has a sensitivity weight controlling how much it moves the 10yr yield when you drag its slider. A weight of 0.20 means a 1-unit increase adds 20bp to the 10yr.

How to use: Adjust the multiplier (0% to 300%) if you think the model is over- or under-weighting a factor. Reset weights restores defaults.

What are linkages? Macro factors do not move independently. When inflation spikes, the Fed reacts. When the labor market weakens, recession risk rises. The linkages below capture the most empirically robust of these relationships. Toggle "Enable dynamic factor linkages" at the top of the model to activate them — dragging one slider will then automatically adjust related sliders.

When to use them: Realistic scenario testing (e.g. "what if CPI prints 5.5%?"). When NOT to use them: Isolating the pure effect of a single variable.

How the model works

This is a delta-sensitivity model anchored to actual US Treasury yields. The model starts from real market levels and calculates how much each factor would move the curve if it shifted from its baseline value.

The math: Implied yield = Actual yield + sum of (weight times delta) for each tenor. Delta is the change in a factor from the baseline.

This approach is grounded in the Adrian-Crump-Moench term structure decomposition used by the NY Fed.

Model limitations

Linear approximation: Real yield dynamics are non-linear in extreme stress.

Factor correlation: Real factors move together; this model treats them independently.

Not a trading model: This is a scenario analysis and communication tool.
